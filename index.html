Protect the environment and recycle your electronic waste and lithium batteries with us.

Li Batteries

  • Li-ion batteries are commonly used in portable electronic devices.
  • When disposing of Li-ion batteries, it's important to do so properly to minimize their impact on the environment.
  • Li-ion batteries can pose a fire hazard if not disposed of properly.
  • Li-ion batteries should be taken to a recycling center or a designated drop-off location.
  • Recycling Li-ion batteries helps to reduce the amount of hazardous materials that end up in landfills or incinerators and can recover valuable materials for reuse.

E Waste

  • E-waste refers to electronic products that have reached the end of their useful life.
  • Many electronic devices contain hazardous materials that can pose a threat to human health and the environment if not disposed of properly.
  • Recycling e-waste helps to reduce the amount of hazardous materials that end up in landfills or incinerators and can recover valuable materials for reuse.
  • It's important to properly dispose of e-waste and follow local laws and regulations related to e-waste disposal
  • Local recycling programs and drop-off locations can help individuals and businesses properly dispose of e-waste.
